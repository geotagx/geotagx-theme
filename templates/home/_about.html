The GeoTag-X pilot project will teach volunteers to perform in-depth analysis of

photos , video and audio coming out of a disaster.


This pilot project calls out to all Citizen Scientists to help
filter , categorise and map this data.


What is GeoTag-X ?

Disaster response efforts have to analyse massive amounts of data that are produced during a crisis. An overwhelmingly difficult task.

GeoTag-X is a research project that will assess if we can teach you, our volunteers, to recognise the important information in a photo and create relevant, structured datasets that can be used by those working on the ground in the response. In the end, we want to know if GeoTag-X could be a useful tool in an operational context.

In order to assess this we have set up a series of pilot projects covering different disaster related events. Disasters could include earthquakes , floods, and hurricanes , along with more slow moving events such as drought , climate change , and pollution . Analysis in our pilot projects therefore could cover topics as diverse as environmental conditions , health, agriculture, and engineering.

Who are our Volunteers ?

Anyone can volunteer with GeoTag-X. Our volunteers provide real value to the different projects on GeoTag-X by testing the applications, analysing photos, and providing feedback. Their work and the data they create is crucial to the success of GeoTag-X. While GeoTag-X is not currently an operational tool, without volunteers testing applications and analysing photos in this development period we would never progress beyond a simple idea into something that can provide concrete help to disaster responders. Along the way we hope the volunteers themselves will acquire new skills and a better understanding of what a disaster is and what it means for those caught up in it.

Other ways to participate

We welcome collaborators for new GeoTag-X projects, such as organisations or researchers addressing a particular crisis. You can send us an email if you have an idea for a project on GeoTag-X. We are researching how visitors learn through their interaction with GeoTag-X and the projects on it, to be part of this project make sure you sign up for an account and accept to be part of the research project. If you are interested in why we are doing this you can learn more at Citizen Cyberlab.
